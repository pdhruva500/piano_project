Playing Piano At Supportive Housing

A narrative of sharing music with the community

‚Üê Back to Articles

This is a narrative of my experiences sharing music with a community of formerly unhoused people.

Music has always been a way for me to connect with others and bring joy to different communities. When I had the opportunity to play piano at supportive housing facilities, I discovered the profound impact that live music can have on people who are rebuilding their lives.

These experiences taught me that music transcends circumstances and speaks directly to the human spirit. Whether playing classical pieces, jazz standards, or popular songs, I witnessed how melodies could lift spirits, evoke memories, and create moments of peace and joy.

Each performance was a reminder of why I became a music teacher in the first place: to share the transformative power of music with as many people as possible.
